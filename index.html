Sitti's Hearth: A Palestinian Tale

Ahlan wa sahlan! I am Sitti, a Palestinian grandmother from the village of Dimra. The rhythms of our lives here are woven with stories passed down through generations. Here, in the humble embrace of Dimra, I invite you to join me on a journey through our cherished ways.

Would you join me in embracing our traditions and lending a hand with daily chores, dear friend?

Step into Sitti's world in the village of Dimra. Help Sitti with daily tasks, cook traditional meals, and tend to the village garden. Your choices shape the story, revealing the rich tapestry of Palestinian heritage. Immerse yourself in a heartfelt adventure celebrating unity, resilience, and the enduring spirit of community.

Shukran, habibi! Our village, crowned by ancient minarets and adorned with homes crafted from the sun-kissed earth, echoes with the whispers of generations past. Your willingness to join reflects the spirit of 'sumud,' our steadfast perseverance, deeply rooted within our Palestinian heritage.

Dear one, as the sun sets upon our beloved Dimra, my humble adobe dims with the fading light. Would you kindly assist me in illuminating our surroundings? Look to the lantern resting on the table; it awaits your touch, just as the spirit of our ancestors beckons for unity and radiance.

Double-click on the lantern nearby; it holds the secret to our guiding light.

The aroma of Maqluba dances with memories of unity, while the sweetness of Knafeh mirrors the warmth of our gatherings. In our Palestinian culture, gathering to share food is akin to sharing blessings. As the Quran teaches, 'And feed with food the needy, the orphan, and the captive' [Quran 76:8].

Alas, in the rush of the day, I seem to have misplaced the food meant to share with our beloved neighborhood children.

Hover over the dishes to help me gather them.

Barakallahu feek. Your kindness and willingness to assist me warm my old heart, dear friend. Now, would you honor me further? You have a choice: help tend to the garden, where each blossom speaks of hope and perseverance, or join me in the art of sewing, where threads intertwine like our stories.

Should you choose to delve into the garden, nurturing life from the soil, know that your touch breathes life into our land. And if your path leads to sewing, together we'll spin tales through the delicate art of needle and thread. Your decision, my love, will be yet another chapter in the tapestry of our shared experiences.

Ah, the garden, where the Earth whispers its secrets and life blooms in harmony. I find solace among the blossoms and the olive trees, our revered companions, cherished symbols of our Palestinian heritage. Their roots run deep, like the stories etched into the soul of our land. As the Quran tells us, 'And We sent down from the sky blessed water whereby We caused gardens and harvest-grain to grow [Quran, 50:9].

The garden awaits your touch, like the gentle rain upon parched land, a blessing from above. Let the water breathe life into their waiting souls and nourish them as the rain does the earth.

Hover over the seeds and grant them the nourishment they crave.

Shukran, habibi! Your act of kindness resonates deeply. As the Quran shares in Surah Ar-Rum, 'And He gave you clothing that protects you from heat and other things, and coats of mail that protect you in your battles.'

Our Palestinian culture finds its expression in the vibrant patterns of the Keffiyeh, a symbol of solidarity, and the graceful folds of the Hijab, a testament to modesty and strength. Although my heart delights in crafting garments for my precious grandchildren, my hands often falter with the weight of time.

Would you lend a hand, gentle soul?

Double click on the sewing machine to bring forth new clothes for my little ones.

Your kindness knows no bounds, my love. In these moments when hearts converge, I humbly invite you to join me in making dua for Palestine, our beloved land. In the embrace of our traditions, dua is a cherished act, an echo of hope that reverberates across our land.

As the Quran reminds us in Surah Al-Baqarah, 'Allah does not burden a soul beyond that it can bear' [Quran, 2:286]. Our land, our people, we hold them dear in our hearts, and in our prayers, we seek peace and justice.

Will you join me in this heartfelt prayer?

اللهم ألهم أهل فلسطين القوة والصبر، وارزقهم السلام والأمان. احفظهم من كل سوء وانصرهم على الظلم. اللهم اجعل السلام يعم الأرض وأرنا فيها العدل والإنصاف. آمين.

O Allah, grant the people of Palestine strength and patience. Bestow upon them peace and security. Protect them from all harm and grant them victory over oppression. O Allah, spread peace on Earth and show us justice and fairness. Ameen.
